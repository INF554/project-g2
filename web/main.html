We begin by exploring the overall operation of the shelter. We use 3 Line Charts to display a number of incoming, outgoing, and leftover animals for each month. The seasonal pattern can be easily spotted; the number of incoming & outgoing tend to increase and peak out around May-Jun, then decline and bottom out near Dec-Jan. Overall, the number of leftover animals are pretty stable. By separating the chart by animalType, we found that even though most incoming/outgoing animals are dogs, their numbers are relatively stable throughout the years. Most fluctuations are actually come from cats.

Found location of intake animals is heuristic, which can let people explore potential patterns in finding homeless animals. We use geomap to display top-10 frequent area in finding animals. Besides, people can choose to see frequent area base on total record, different animals, years, seasons, time in a day . The opacity of orange represent how much animals are found here and people can click on the frequent area to see the exact number.

There are multiple attributes to discribe animals. Here we use sankey diagram to display the proportion of these attribute. This graph includes both attribute for incoming animals and for outgoing animals . The graph is also include one nodes column for adoption infromation, providing people with further exploration, like how some feature of animal effect it been adopt or not.

We narrow down our focus to dog & cat. We interest in how long does it takes for the pet owners to take their pet back. Small Multiple is used to display a relationship between the Animal's age (upon intake) and the Median days that the animals with this age range stayed until they were returned to their owner. For dogs, as they grow older, their owners tend to take them back sooner. This may suggest a tighter relationship between owner & dog as time go by. Surprisingly, such pattern is not observed in cats. Additionally, cat owners tend to take longer time to return their cat backs. By inspecting the number of cats in each age range, we noticed that this may be because of an insufficient number of records.

How about dog & cat that got their new owner? The relationship between the Animal's age and the Staying duration is explored in a similar manner. Compare to the above charts, it takes a longer time for both to find a new adopter. The number of adopted cats also decreases dramatically as they grow older. This can be a bit unexpected consider the fact that cats have lower maintenance cost and usually live longer.

Lastly, we found that some animals were taken by the shelter multiple times. This may indicates a mistreatment of pets. We provide a list of these animal, along with their additional information. We use Calendar to mark the event when they came to, and went out of the shelter. Users can also investigate intakeCondition and outgoingCondition by hovering on the corresponding cell or selecting 'showAllToolTips'. Geographical Map is also used to mark the location where they were found. The number in the tooltips and markers indicates the order of Intake event. By clicking on the Intake cell, the map will focus to the associated event. While we expect that animals should be found in a similar area where they were previously discovered, some were found in a very unusual, distant location.
